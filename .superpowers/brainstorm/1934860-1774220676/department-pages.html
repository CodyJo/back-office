Department Pages — How should they connect?

The department pages (QA, SEO, ADA, etc.) are currently isolated islands with no nav, no product filter, and no way to move between them. How should they work?

A

Shared Shell

Every department page gets the same persistent top bar: product selector + department tabs (QA | SEO | ADA | Compliance | Monetization | Product) + link back to HQ. Switching departments is a tab click. Switching products re-filters findings instantly. The URL carries both: /ada?product=selah. Each department keeps its own specialized layout below the shell.

Best practice: Persistent navigation + persistent context (product filter) = users always know where they are and never lose their place.

Pros: Consistent UX, product context preserved, easy cross-department navigation
Cons: Need to add product filtering to all 6 department pages (we just built multi-repo support, so this is feasible)
B

Single Page App

Kill the separate department pages. Everything lives inside the HQ page as expandable sections. Click a department score card or matrix cell and it expands inline to show the full department detail (findings table, score breakdown, charts). No page navigation at all — just expand/collapse sections on one page.

Best practice: Fewer pages = less context switching. But only works if the data volume stays manageable per section.

Pros: Zero navigation needed, everything in one place, instant context
Cons: Page gets very long, harder to bookmark a specific view, large DOM with 600+ findings
C

Slide-Over Panels

HQ stays as the main page. Clicking a department card or matrix cell opens a slide-over panel (right side, 60-70% width) showing the department detail. The HQ stays visible underneath. Close the panel to go back. Product context is inherited from HQ's selector automatically. Multiple panels can stack.

Best practice: Used by Linear, Notion, and other modern tools. Great for "peek and go back" workflows.

Pros: Never lose HQ context, quick drill-in/out, product filter automatic
Cons: Complex to build well, slide-over can feel cramped for data-heavy views like QA (60+ findings)
