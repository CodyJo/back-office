HQ Dashboard — Which approach feels right?

Three different philosophies for the main landing page. Click the one that resonates.

A

Executive Summary

One glanceable screen. Product selector at top, then a single row of 6 department score cards. Below that, a "Needs Attention" feed showing only critical/high findings sorted by urgency. No org chart, no manual log, no delivery section. Click any department card to drill into its full page.

Best practice: Operations dashboards should answer "what needs my attention right now?" in under 5 seconds. Everything else is a click away.

Keeps: Product filter, department scores, urgent findings
Removes: HR roster, delivery acceptance, manual log, employee scores
Adds: Trend sparklines on each card (score over last 5 scans)
B

Product-First Hub

Landing page shows product cards (Selah, Analogify, Cordivent, TNBM, codyjo.com, Back Office). Each card shows a mini health bar across all 6 departments. Click a product to see its dedicated view with all department scores and findings for just that product. Departments become a secondary navigation within each product.

Best practice: When managing multiple products, the first question is usually "which product needs work?" not "which department?"

Keeps: Department scores (nested under products)
Removes: HR roster, delivery acceptance, manual log
Adds: Per-product landing pages, cross-product comparison view
C

Hybrid: Summary + Pivot

Top section: product selector + 6 department score cards (like A). Middle section: a smart grid that shows the intersection — products as rows, departments as columns, with color-coded score cells. Click any cell to jump directly to that product+department view. Bottom: "Needs Attention" feed. This gives you both the department view AND product view on one page.

Best practice: Matrix views let operators spot patterns (e.g., "ADA is red across 3 products" or "Selah is red across 4 departments") that single-axis views miss.

Keeps: Product filter, department scores
Removes: HR roster, delivery acceptance, manual log
Adds: Product × Department matrix, cell-level drill-through
